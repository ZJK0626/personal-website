Hello.

Junke Zhao

Incoming Ph.D. Student in Computer Science

University of Texas at Dallas

I'm incoming PhD student in Computer Science at DE4M Lab, UT Dallas, advised by Prof. Liang He. Before this, I obtained my Master of Science degree in Computational Design from Carnegie Mellon University in May 2025, and obtained my Bachelor of Architecture degree from Southeast University in June 2023.

My research interests focus on the intersection of design and technology, with a focus on exploring computational design, digital fabrication, tangible interaction, and ubiquitous computing in the field of human-computer interaction.

People spend increasingly more time immersed in digital technologies, but I have always believed that the tangible, perceivable physical world is what fundamentally shapes our lived experiences. Rather than viewing built environments as static backdrops, I see them as active media that can sense, respond, and adapt to human needs. My research integrates interactive and adaptive systems into everyday physical spaces, while making the design tools used to create them more accessible. My work spans three interconnected directions:

Spaces that Understand

I investigate how physical spaces can interpret occupants' behaviors through embedded sensing and AI, and adapt in real time without requiring explicit user commands.

People who Shape

I explore AI-assisted design tools that allow non-experts to express spatial intentions in natural language and see them translated into actionable physical changes.

Realities that Blend

I explore how spatial computing can augment physical environments through context-aware virtual layers that respect real-world properties and constraints.
